KIXLAB · Interactive Alignment

I am a fourth year PhD candidate in the School of Computing at KAIST. I work with Juho Kim as a member of KIXLAB.

My work falls in the intersection of HCI and NLP, focusing on interactive alignment of AI models. Specifically, I explore how to disentangle natural language into interactive components that empower users to iterate on their intents and audit the model's behaviors.

DiscoverLLM: From Executing Intents to Discovering Them

Preprint 2026
PDFarXiv
Tae Soo Kim, Yoonjoo Lee, Jaesang Yu, John Joon Young Chung, Juho Kim

Users are often ambiguous in their requests to LLMs not because they have unexpressed intents, but because they have not yet formed their intents — they must explore outcomes to discover what they want. DiscoverLLM introduces a generalizable framework that trains LLMs to help users form and discover their intents, using a novel user simulator that models cognitive state with a hierarchy of intents that progressively concretize as the model surfaces relevant options.

Evalet: Evaluating Large Language Models by Fragmenting Outputs into Functions

(Cond. Accept) CHI 2026
PDFWebsiteCodearXiv
Tae Soo Kim, Heechan Lee, Yoonjoo Lee, Joseph Seering, Juho Kim

Evalet addresses the opacity of LLM-based evaluations by decomposing model outputs into fragment-level functions — automatically dissecting responses into fragments and interpreting the function each serves relative to evaluation criteria, so instead of opaque scores, practitioners can see exactly which parts of an output drove the evaluation and why, enabling them to validate and refine their evaluation setups with transparency.

CUPID: Evaluating Personalized and Contextualized Alignment of LLMs from Interactions

COLM 2025
PDFWebsiteCodearXiv
Tae Soo Kim, Yoonjoo Lee, Yoonah Park, Jiho Kim, Young-Ho Kim, Juho Kim

CUPID presents a framework for evaluating how well LLMs personalize and contextualize their responses through multi-turn interactions, introducing metrics for assessing personalized alignment — measuring whether models truly learn from user feedback within a conversation or simply default to generic responses. The work highlights the gap between static alignment benchmarks and the dynamic, personalized interactions users actually have with AI systems.

One vs. Many: Comprehending Accurate Information from Multiple Erroneous and Inconsistent AI Generations

FAccT 2024
PDFarXivACM DL
Yoonjoo Lee, Kihoon Son, Tae Soo Kim, Jisu Kim, John Joon Young Chung, Eytan Adar, Juho Kim

An investigation into how people comprehend accurate information when presented with multiple erroneous and inconsistent AI-generated outputs. The study explores strategies users employ to identify reliable information across conflicting AI generations and provides design implications for AI systems that present multiple outputs.

EvalLM: Interactive Evaluation of Large Language Model Prompts on User-Defined Criteria

CHI 2024
PDFWebsiteVideoACM DLarXivCode
Tae Soo Kim, Yoonjoo Lee, Jamin Shin, Young-Ho Kim, Juho Kim

EvalLM is an interactive system for evaluating and comparing LLM prompts against user-defined criteria: users specify what matters to them in natural language (e.g., tone, accuracy, creativity), and EvalLM automatically evaluates multiple prompt variants side-by-side, surfacing tradeoffs and enabling structured, criteria-driven prompt engineering beyond subjective judgment.

Cells, Generators, and Lenses: Design Framework for Object-Oriented Interaction with Large Language Models

UIST 2023
PDFWebsiteCodeACM DL
Tae Soo Kim, Yoonjoo Lee, Minsuk Chang, Juho Kim

This paper introduces a design framework with three primitives for object-oriented interaction with LLMs: Cells (discrete units of content users can select and manipulate), Generators (which produce new content from cells using LLM capabilities), and Lenses (which provide different views and transformations of generated content) — enabling users to decompose complex tasks into manageable, reusable interaction patterns rather than treating LLM interactions as opaque prompt-response exchanges.

Papeos: Augmenting Research Papers with Talk Videos

UIST 2023
PDFarXivACM DLDemo
Tae Soo Kim, Matt Latzke, Jonathan Bragg, Amy X. Zhang, Joseph Chee Chang

Papeos augments research papers with talk videos by automatically aligning paper sections with video segments using multimodal alignment techniques, enabling readers to click any section of a paper to play the corresponding conference talk explanation — with synchronized highlighting and a dual-view interface that makes dense academic papers more approachable through the complementary medium of video presentation.

PDFWebsiteVideoACM DL

Stylette: Styling the Web with Natural Language

CHI 2022 Honorable Mention Award
PDFWebsiteVideoACM DL
Tae Soo Kim, DaEun Choi, Yoonseo Choi, Juho Kim

Stylette is a browser extension that enables users to style web pages using natural language — users describe desired changes conversationally (e.g., "make the header bigger") and Stylette translates these into precise CSS modifications through an interactive feedback loop with live previews, bridging the gap between design intent and CSS implementation for both non-developers and professionals.
